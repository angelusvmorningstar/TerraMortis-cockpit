## What went well

1. **Connection isolation as a structural safety mechanism.** `lib/connect.mjs` is the single DB access point; the locality guard makes "cannot touch production" a property of the code, not a promise. Proven today against a live prod read (the seed sat *outside* that boundary by design, used read-only).
2. **Pre-planning a known failure mode paid off literally.** Story 1.3 predicted the ISP would block SRV DNS and pre-authored a non-SRV fallback ladder. At seed time we pulled the non-SRV seed-list string straight from the suite `.env` and it worked first try. Anticipating the failure before hitting it is the repeatable win.
3. **State-the-gap discipline survived into real output.** The gap markers (`[none on record]`, never omit/invent) and the verbatim standing instruction — the entire purpose of the tool — are present and correct in the generated pack.
4. **Clean BMAD cadence.** PRD → architecture → epics → sharded stories → one-commit-per-story → QA each. Discipline never slipped.

## Challenges and lessons

1. **A story whose proof is "run it live" must own its environment — or it strands the chain.** Story 2.6 carried a QA APPROVE but was *unproven* for the whole interval until today, because it can't run headless (`buildCharacterIndex` connects) and there was no local Mongo or seeded sandbox. Story 1.3 only ever produced a *document*; the seed was written and reviewed but never executed, and that single un-run step is what left 2.6 in limbo.
   - **Lesson / action:** when a story's acceptance criterion is an empirical run, provisioning the environment (install + seed) belongs *inside* that story's tasks, not assumed as ambient. Otherwise "done on paper" hides "never actually run."
2. **Known debt, carried on purpose:** two Mongo connections per run (`buildCharacterIndex` opens its own; the orchestrator opens a second). Acceptable for a once-per-cycle local CLI; logged for a future inject-the-connection refactor. Explicitly not worth fixing now.
3. **Reference content is an authoring task, not just code.** Stories 2.1/2.3 surfaced that disambiguation notes (the three Renés; Astrid/Odeliese/Elise) are curated reference content, not auto-detectable by the forename-collision pass — which is why the `disambiguations` collection exists. Authoring the chronicle reference is real work distinct from the generator.

## Next epic preparation — Epic 3: Validate the grounding

- The baseline exists: `Downtime_Hallucination_Audit.md` — ~13 named instances (8 verified + 4 partial + an under-count caveat), PRD baseline of fifteen, classified by fabrication type, drawn from DT1/DT3/DT4 processing.

**Recommended validation method (decided by facilitator at Project Lead's request):** re-draft the **already-audited material**, not a fresh live cycle.
- The audit *is* the "before" — same inputs, controlled comparison, real delta.
- A fresh DT5 draft has no before-count and so cannot produce a delta; use DT5 only as a supplementary "does it stay clean going forward" check.
- **Bonus output:** the re-audit will reveal which fabrication classes the Phase-1 pack *cannot* yet prevent (e.g. the fabricated Charles letter and the invented Court departures need correspondence/travel fields the pack does not carry — Phase 2, FR17/FR18/FR26-28). That failure list becomes Phase 2 scope for free.
